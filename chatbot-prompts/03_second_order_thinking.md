# 🚀 Second Order Thinking

## 🧩 Purpose
This prompt helps you look beyond the immediate effects of a decision to identify the "ripples" or long-term consequences that are often overlooked.

---

## 🧠 Prompt Template

```text
Act as a systems thinker and risk analyst. I am considering the following action: [DESCRIBE YOUR PROPOSED DECISION OR ACTION]

Help me perform a "Second-Order Thinking" analysis:
1. First-Order Effects: What are the immediate, obvious results of this action?
2. Second-Order Effects: What happens *as a result* of the first-order effects? (Look 1-6 months ahead)
3. Third-Order Effects: What are the long-term systemic consequences? (Look 1-2 years ahead)
4. Identify unintended negative consequences and potential "hidden" benefits.

Be critical and look for where this decision might fail or cause friction in other areas of my life/business.
```

---

## 📌 How to Use
Use this when you have a "good idea" but want to make sure it doesn't create bigger problems later. Simply fill in the decision and paste.

---

## 💡 Example Input
"I am planning to automate all customer support responses using an AI chatbot to save money on staff."

---

## ✅ Expected Output
An analysis showing that while you save money (1st order), you might see a drop in customer brand loyalty due to lack of human touch (2nd order), potentially leading to higher customer acquisition costs later (3rd order).
